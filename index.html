About HackBlagoevgrad

HackBlagoevgrad is a week-long IT forum which aims at combining the formats of a conference consisting of several presentations and workshops given by professionals in the field, and a hackathon during which the participants work in teams on developing certain technological products.

The idea behind its organization was born when a group of young people studying and working in the IT field got together. Since we were all related in one way or another to the city of Blagoevgrad, we saw the dire need of bringing such a forum to the local audience. After thorough research and extensive planning today we present to you the shape of what was once just a whim and will soon enough come to life.

The first edition of will take place in the week November 14-20, 2016 on the Campus of the American University in Bulgaria.

Schedule

As scheduled, the first HackBlagoevgrad event will go in the following way: Monday through Thursday (November 14-17, 2016): Starting at 7:30PM companies’ professionals give presentations/workshops in one of the aulas in Balkanski Academic Center, AUBG Campus.

Friday (November 18, 2016): Around 6PM participants and mentors gather for the kick-off of the hackathon weekend. The participants discuss their ideas and form teams (if they have not done so previously at the time of registration or during the presentations’/workshops’ days). After that, the teams head for their personal workspaces in the Sports Hall of the ABF Student Center, AUBG Campus, and start working.

Saturday (November 19, 2016): The participants continue working on their ideas throughout the whole day/night and the mentors help them.

Sunday (November 20, 2016): The participants finish up their projects. In the evening participants, mentors, and spectators gather in Dr. Carl Djerassi Theater Hall, AUBG Campus. Every team presents what they have done in front of the jury and an open audience. After that comes the voting and the jury announces the winners who receive prizes.

Organizers

We, the people behind the scenes of HackBlagoevgrad, are a group of young enthusiasts who believe in the future of technology and in the abilities of our fellows to take the initiative and make a change.

Currently, the main responsibility for the organization of the week-long event is shared between the Computer Science Students Union (CSSU) at the American University in Bulgaria, the High School of Mathematics “Acad. Sergey Pavlovich Korolyov” in Blagoevgrad, and other devotees to our common mission. Clearly, our dreams would not have become a reality without the special help and support granted by our partners from HackBulgaria.
